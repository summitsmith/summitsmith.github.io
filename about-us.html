Profile

Elidana Children's hope is a registered children no-governmental organisation that was established in 2019 as the only alternative to the growning child neglect in the community due to some being orphaned and abject poverty at household level.
Elidana children's Hope was established by Elijah Mukwaya who grew up without parental care and love. Elijah gew up without neither a father nor a mother. His father died when Elijah was not yet born and his mother died a maternal death. Elijah grew up with her maternal grandmother from day one up to today. Despite the suffering he went through , Elijah could feel pity seeing other children suffering, not going to school yet he was not also schooling. When Elijah completed his Ordinary level education he went to acquire photography skills and today he is one of the best photgraphers and videographers in Kayabwe town.
Initially in march 2019, it first begun as an informal project called ELIJAH'S help for Orphans at the time then Eijah was staying and carring for the vulnerable orphans from his home place but later on he was adviced to resettle these children to their relatives since the government did not gazetted it as a clear home for children, it was this moment that Elijah learnt the fact that to stay with orphans and needy children was procedural and recommendations straight from the government. In November 2019 the procedures begun and the former name was changed to ELIDANA CHILDREN'S HOPE. The organization is now formally incorporated, we submitted the complete file to the ministry of internal affairs for registration under the national burreau of NGOs after receiving a great recommendation from the ministry of Gender Labor and Social Development.

Our Mission

The mission of ELIDANA CHILDREN'S HOPE is to love and serve orphans and other vulnerable children through creating sustainable developmental opportunities for them.

Our Vision

  • To end tears.
  • To have a free child suffering world.

Objectives

  • To provide basic needs to vulnerable children(education, food, medical care, shelter and clothing).
  • To equip youth with knowledge and gainful employable skills for self-reliance such as photography, cinematography and more other skills.
